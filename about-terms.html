About & Terms

This site provides free machining, geometry, and calculator tools for reference use. The calculators are meant to help with everyday shop math, layout, and planning, but they are not a substitute for your own verification, inspection, or professional judgment.

Safety and accuracy

Always check the results before using them. Verify your inputs, units, decimal places, rounding settings, signs, offsets, setup, workholding, tooling, and machine motion. Before running any CNC program or using calculated values at the machine, simulate, dry-run, single-block, prove out, and inspect as appropriate for your machine and process.

Do not rely on this site as the only check before cutting material, moving a machine, editing offsets, or making safety-critical decisions. Machines, tools, fixtures, and parts can be damaged if numbers are wrong or used incorrectly.
